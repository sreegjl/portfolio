Key equations

Velocity\(v = v_0 + at\)
Position\(x = x_0 + v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2\)
No-time\(v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x\)
Average\(\Delta x = \tfrac{1}{2}(v_0 + v)t\)
Horizontal\(v_x = v_0\cos\theta\)
Vertical\(v_y = v_0\sin\theta - gt\)
Gravity\(g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2\)

On a velocity-time graph, displacement is represented by:

A The slope of the line
B The area under the curve
C The y-intercept
D The curvature of the line
The area under a v-t graph gives displacement. The slope of a v-t graph gives acceleration.

Question 3

A ball is thrown straight up with \(v_0 = 20\text{ m/s}\). At the highest point, the ball's velocity and acceleration are:

A \(v = 0\), \(a = 0\)
B \(v = 0\), \(a = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2\) downward
C \(v = 20\text{ m/s}\), \(a = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2\) downward
D \(v = 0\), \(a = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2\) upward
At the peak, velocity is momentarily zero, but acceleration due to gravity is always \(9.8\text{ m/s}^2\) downward throughout the entire flight.
